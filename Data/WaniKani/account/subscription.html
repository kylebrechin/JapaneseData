The (Not-so) Fine Print

How Your Payment Information is Handled

WaniKani uses Stripe for the payment processor. Stripe is well-regarded in the payment process industry and is certified to PCI Service Provider Level 1. This is the most stringent level of certification available. Stripe and WaniKani force HTTPS to ensure your payment data is transmitted safely. HTTPS encrypts your information and verifies the integrity of traffic between your browser, WaniKani, and Stripe.

We do not store your payment information on our servers. All payment information is sent to Stripe, which is then encrypted on disk with AES-256. WaniKani is given a unique identification key, which is how we reference to your payment information when requesting charges with Stripe.

More information on Stripe's security practices can be found on their website. Third-party information on Stripe can be found on its Wikipedia page.

Footnotes

  1. Recurring Payments ↩︎

    WaniKani will charge you at the beginning of a subscription period. A period begins on the date when a subscription is added and ends a month or year after the date, depending on the plan selected. Refer to the generated invoice for more information.

    If you do not wish to have your subscription on recurring charge (Month or Annual) simply cancel the subscription anytime during the subscription period under Billing. The account will remain active until the end of the subscription period. At the end of your subscription you will only have access to the free levels (up to level 3). Don't fret! If you decide to subscribe again you'll be back to where you have left off.

  2. Estimates and Proration ↩︎

    WaniKani charges in United States Dollars (USD). If your card issuing bank's default currency is not USD then your bank and/or payment processor may charge a conversion and/or foreign transaction fees in addition to our subscription fee.

    If you are currently a subscriber and changing plans you will be prorated for the amount unused, based on what you were charged on the current period's invoice. The prorated amount will be used to pay the new invoice. Prorate is calculated to the second. The new subscription period begins on the date it is added. Charges and invoicing can be viewed under Billing.

    For example, if you are half-way on a monthly subscription (USD 9/month, the amount paid on the invoice) and wish to upgrade to an annual plan (USD 89/year), a proration of USD 4.50 will occur. This will go towards the first invoice for the annual subscription, for a total charge of USD 84.50. Subsequent invoices for the annual subscription would be for the full USD 89.00.

    Another use case is when transitioning from a more expensive plan to a less expensive plan, such as going from Annual to Month. If the prorated amount exceeds the Month's rate your WaniKani account will be credited the difference. This is listed as line item "Account credit" under "Payment information". The line item is only displayed if your account has credits. Subsequent charges will deduct from the account credit fund until it is exhausted, at which point the card on record will be used to cover the charges.

    Proration listed when Lifetime is selected is calculated on the timestamp and only good at the timestamp. Refresh the page to see the most accurate proration.

    Proration extends to all plans, except going from Lifetime to any plan.
